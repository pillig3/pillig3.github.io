About Me

Welcome, Wanderer of the Web!

I am a senior math major at Carleton College (no, not that Carleton) in scenic Northfield, Minnesota. I'm currently doing my comps (capstone project) on the reducibility of polynomial iterates. Here's an interesting fact: the polynomial \( x^2 - x - 1 \) is well-known for one of its roots, the golden ratio \( \phi \), but it is also one of just nine polynomials \( x^2 + ax + b \) with \( a, b \) integers satisfying \( |a| \le 100,000 \) and \( |b| \le 1,000,000,000 \) such that \( f(f(x)) \) is irreducible but \( f(f(f(x))) \) is reducible! For comparison, there are 67 polynomials with \( |a|,|b| \le 100 \) that have \( f(x) \) irreducible but \( f(f(x)) \) reducible. Why are newly reducible third iterates so sparse? Why is the minimal polynomial of \( \phi \) one of them? Are there infinitely many? These are among the questions I'm exploring.

Outside of math, I also enjoy writing, programming, board games, hiking, origami, biking, and cross-country skiing (see below).
