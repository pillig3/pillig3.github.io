A Golden Equation

Solutions to the differential equation \(y'' - y' - y = 0\). The characteristic polynomial for this equation is \( \lambda^2-\lambda-1 \), which has the roots \( \varphi \) and \( 1-\varphi \), where \(\varphi\) is the golden ratio. Thus all of the solutions have the form \( y=c_1 e^{\varphi t} + c_2 e^{(1-\varphi)t} \). This graph shows the solutions where \(c_1,c_2\) are integers between \(-5\) and \(5\). Each solution is colored with a mix of red and blue: more red the larger \(c_1\) is, and more blue the larger \(c_2\) is.
Mathemtica:

sols=Flatten[Table[c1 E^((1/2-Sqrt[5]/2)t)+c2 E^((1/2+Sqrt[5]/2)t),{c1,-5,5},{c2,-5,5}]];
Plot[sols,{t,-3,2},PlotRange->{-15,15},PlotStyle->Flatten[Table[RGBColor[(1+9i/10)/10,0,(1+9j/10)/10],{i,0,10},{j,0,10}]]]
