This page gives a brief introduction to my comps (comprehensive senior project), and a few neat tidbits.

This project focuses on the reducibility of polynomial iterates. If \( f(x) \) is an arbitrary polynomial, define the \(n\)th iterate of \(f\) to be

\( f^n(x) = \underbrace{f(f(...(f(x))))}_{n\ f\text{'s}}. \)

We also say that \( f^n(x) \) is newly reducible, or that \(f\) has a newly reducible \(n\)th iterate, when all of \( f(x), f^2(x), ..., f^{n-1}(x) \) are irreducible, but \( f^n(x) \) is reducible (over \(\mathbb{Q}\), although we can study this for any field). Note that if some iterate \( f^n(x) \) is reducible, then \( f^n(x)=h_1(x)h_2(x) \), so we also have

\( f^{n+1}(x) = f^n(f(x)) = h_1(f(x))h_2(f(x)), \)

so \( f^{n+1}(x) \) is also reducible. Therefore, we're searching only for the first time an iterate of \( f \) is reducible, because all iterates after that will also be reducible.

Here's an example: the polynomial \( f(x) = x^2 - x - 1 \) is a famous one, being the minimal polynomial of the golden ratio \( \varphi \). It is irreducible, since \( \varphi \) is irrational. Now let's iterate it:

\( \begin{align*} f^2(x) &= x^4 - 2x^3 - 2x^2 + 3x + 1 &\text{ is irreducible.} \\ f^3(x) &= (x^4 - 3x^3 + 4x - 1) (x^4 - x^3 - 3x^2 + x + 1) &\text{ is reducible!} \end{align*} \)

So this polynomial has a newly reducible third iterate. This makes it very rare; there are only eight other integer polynomials of the form \( x^2 + ax + b \), with \( |a| \le 10^5, |b| \le 10^9 \), that have newly reducible third iterates. It's not hard to show that there are infinitely many rational polynomials of the form \( x^2 + ax + b \) with newly reducible second iterates, but before we began it wasn't known whether or not there are infinitely many with newly reducible third iterates.

So, our preliminary questions were:
How many polynomials of the above form have newly reducible third iterates?
Why is the minimal polynomial of \( \varphi \) one of them?

In the time since, we've tackled some additional questions: (the last three are still unsolved)
How many rational polynomials of the form \( x^3 + ax^2 + bx + c \) have newly reducible second iterates?
How many rational polynomials of the form \( x^4 + ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d \) have newly reducible second iterates?
Given a degree \(d\), how many rational, monic, degree-\(d\) polynomials have newly reducible second iterates?
Are there any rational polynomials with newly reducible fourth iterate?
Given \( n\geq 4 \), are there any rational polynomials with newly reducible \(n\)th iterate?
